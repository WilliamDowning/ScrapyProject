    Awards and Scholarships

    Application dates are November 1st - March 15th for most scholarships.

    We are pleased to announce the availability of many scholarship awards for academic year 2020-2021. Construction Management majors (BS, MS and Certificate students) are all eligible to apply. Each scholarship award, and the amount, will be based on actual funds available. Interested students are invited and encouraged to complete the application process. All applicants must be currently enrolled as full-time students (12 credit minimum for BS and 8 credit minimum for MS) and must be in good academic standing.

    • Available Scholarships 

      AECA Scholarship

      This scholarship will be awarded to a student persuing a BS in Construction Management and plans to work in an electrical contracting firm in Atlanta.

      Amount: $2,000
      Scholarships Available: 1

      Choate Scholarship for New Leaders in Construction Management

      Scholarship provides financial assistance to full- or part-time undergraduate or graduate students majoring in Construction Management, with a minimum GPA of 2.5. Preference is given to students who are veterans of, or active reservists in, the United States Military.

      Amount: Based on Financial Need
      Scholarships Available: Varies

      CMIAB Scholarship

      The Construction Management Industry Advisory Board (CMIAB) offers four scholarships, awarded based on merit (GPA), major and performance in curricular and extracurricular activities.

      Amount: $2,000
      Scholarships Available: 4

      Davidson Family Endowed Scholarship

      This scholarship is endowed by Don Davidson, CEO of Inglett and Stubbs and past Chair of the Construction Industry Advisory Board through the Polytechnic Foundation of KSU.

      Amount: $2,500
      Scholarships Available: 1 

      Flagler Foundation Scholarship

      This scholarship will be awarded to two students who demonstrate a need for support and have a GPA of 3.0 or more.

      Amount: $1,000 and $500
      Scholarships Available: 2

      Fluor Scholarship

      This scholarship is available to full-time students majoring in Construction Management. Applicants must have a minimum 3.0 GPA, indicate financial need and include a statement/essay on new technology applications in the construction industry.

      Amount: $1,000
      Scholarships Available: 1

      Golden Hammer Award

      This award is funded jointly by the Professional Construction Estimators Association (PCEA) and the American Society of Professional Estimators (ASPE) Atlanta Chapters. Award receipients will be selected based on service to the profession, extracurricular activities, and merit (GPA).

      Amount: $1,000 and $500
      Scholarships Available: 2

      Greater Atlanta Electrical League Scholarship

      This is a merit-based scholarship that will be awarded to a student pursuing either a B.S. or M.S. in Construction Management.

      Amount: $4,000
      Scholarships Available: 1

      GUCA Scholarship

      The Georgia Utility Contractors Association (GUCA) may award up to three scholarships. This particular scholarship is not in the KSU scholarship system, and needs to be applied for directly through GUCA by filling out this application, and mailing it to the address listed.

      Amount: $1,000
      Scholarships Available: up to 3

      Houzz Scholarship

      This scholarship is open to students studying construction management at the undergraduate or graduate level who wish to pursue residential construction management professionally.

      This particular scholarship is not in the KSU scholarship system, and needs to be applied for directly through HOUZZ.

      Amount: $2,500
      Scholarships Available: 1

      IFMA Scholarship

      The IFMA Atlanta Chapter Scholarship offers scholarships to students who have completed at least two courses in Facilities Management Concentration at the undergraduate or graduate level.

      Amount: $1,000
      Scholarships Available: 10

      Juneau Scholarship

      The Juneau Scholarship is awarded to students in the BS or MS Construction Management programs based on need and minimum GPA.

      Amount: $500, $1,000
      Scholarships Available: 2

      NAWIC Founders’ Scholarship

      Applicants must be currently enrolled full-time in a construction-related degree program at a school in the United States and must have at least one term remaining in a course of study leading to a degree degree in a construction-related field. High school seniors and those pursuing graduate degrees are not eligible. A current cumulative GPA or 3.0 or higher is necessary to be considered for this award.

      This particular scholarship is not in the KSU scholarship system, and needs to be applied for directly through the NAWIC.

      Amount: $500 - $2,500
      Scholarships Available: Varies

      Toy-Mench Scholarships

      To be elligible to apply for this scholarship, students must have completed at least one course in the Specialty Construction concentration and be enrolled in another course. There is a minimum overall KSU GPA requirement of 2.8 for this scholarship.

      Amount: $500
      Scholarships Available: 2

      Whiting-Turner Scholarship

      This is a merit-based scholarship that will be awarded to a student pursuing either a B.S. or M.S. in Construction Management.

      Amount: $2,500
      Scholarships Available: 1

    • How to Apply

      To apply for any of the scholarships listed above, aside from GUCA, Houzz and NAWIC, you must apply through the KSU scholarship application.

      The deadline for academic year 2020-2021 scholarship applications is March 15, 2020. The four-member Scholarship Committee (three CM department representatives and one Advisory Board member) will evaluate the applications received by the deadline and make recommendations to the CM Department Chair. The Chair will announce the names of the scholarship recipients.

      Scholarship awards will be made available to the students' accounts for use toward tuition and fees.

      For GUCA, please send your completed application directly to the address given on the application form before the deadline, or it will not be considered.

      Scholarship recipients will be expected to participate in an awards ceremony and write thank you letters to their scholarship provider.
