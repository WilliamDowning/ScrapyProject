    Employee Well-being Programs

    Health and well-being programs are offered to all KSU faculty and staff! These programs vary each semester and cover a variety of topics including nutrition, weight management, stress management and physical activity. Program details are promoted through the KSU Today Digest email and newsletters to all employees. Be on the lookout for the next program offered! For more information or to suggest a program, please email us.

    • Diabetes Prevention Program

      Kennesaw State University will be offering the Diabetes Prevention Program (DPP) developed by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. This one-year, small group program teaches you how to eat healthy, increase your physical activity and manage your stress in order to prevent or delay type 2 diabetes. 

      For more information contact: Your Employee Well-being team at 470-578-5535 

      To be eligible for the program, you must:

      Have a body mass index (BMI) of 25 or greater   AND                   
      Have prediabetes diagnosed by a blood test OR
      Have a history of gestational diabetes OR
      Screen positive for prediabetes using the American Diabetes Association risk test. 

    • Wellness that Works (WW) At Work

      Kennesaw State University's Wellness that Works (WW) "At Work Program", offers convenient, on-site weekly meetings with a certified Wellness that Works (WW)'s leader. The leader comes to KSU to provide experienced guidance at weekly meetings that fit perfectly into the busy workday. Employees benefit from the proven advantage of group support for successful weight loss with co-workers who understand the workplace environment best.
